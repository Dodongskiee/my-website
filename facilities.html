FACILITIES

Facilities that play a crucial role in ensuring the effective implementation of R.A. 10630 and promoting the welfare of CICLs in the Philippines.

Republic Act No. 10630, also known as the "Juvenile Justice and Welfare Act of 2013," establishes a comprehensive framework for the handling of children in conflict with the law (CICLs) in the Philippines. This includes setting up various facilities to support the implementation of the Act's provisions. Here are some key facilities involved in R.A. 10630:

Intensive Juvenile Intervention and Support Centers (IJISCs):

Purpose: To provide immediate temporary care and intervention for children in conflict with the law (CICLs) aged 12-18 years old who require more intensive support than community-based intervention. Services: These centers offer a range of services, including: Psychological and psychosocial assessment and counseling Educational and vocational training Medical and dental care Recreational activities Legal assistance

Bahay Pag-asa:

Purpose: To serve as temporary shelters for CICLs aged 12-18 years old awaiting disposition of their cases or undergoing community-based intervention programs. Services: Bahay Pag-asa facilities offer basic care and services such as: Safe and secure accommodation Food and clothing Basic medical care Educational and recreational activities

Community-Based Intervention Programs:

Purpose: To provide support and services to CICLs within their own communities, promoting rehabilitation and reintegration without the need for institutionalization. Services: These programs may involve: Counseling and family therapy Educational and vocational training Peer support groups Mentorship and life skills training Mediation and restorative justice initiatives
